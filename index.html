刘南生

Nansheng Liu (1937–2025)
Oil painter. Born 1937 in Quanzhou, Fujian. Trained at Tao Xingzhi's Yucai School in Shanghai, then admitted to the Zhejiang Academy of Fine Arts (now China Academy of Art) in 1953, studying under Li Binghong, Ma Sanhe, Fei Yifu, and Ding Zhengxian. After graduating in 1958, he was assigned to Xinjiang, where he spent twenty-nine years as Associate Professor and Deputy Chair of the Fine Arts Department at Xinjiang Normal University, documenting the life of Uyghur, Kazakh, and Kyrgyz peoples through painting and fieldwork across the Tianshan region. He later worked in Guilin (1987–1992), undertook self-funded art expeditions in Kenya and South Africa (1992–1993), and settled in California in 1994 following the Double Joy Exhibition in San Diego, painting until his passing in October 2025. Selected for the National Art Exhibition six times. Works held by the National Art Museum of China, Guilin Museum, China Academy of Art, and Xinjiang Museum. Member of the China Artists Association and the American Society of Oil Painters.

刘韧

Ren Liu (b. 1969, Urumqi)
Oil painter, son of Nansheng Liu. Born 1969 in Urumqi, Xinjiang. He earned his BFA from the Department of Oil Painting at the China Academy of Art in Hangzhou in 1996 — the same program from which his father had graduated nearly four decades earlier. His exhibition record includes the 15th Annual International Arts Exhibition in San Francisco (1999) and an Honorable Mention at the Zhejiang Oil Painting Show in Ningbo (2005). In 2007–2008, following fieldwork in Tibetan regions, he completed a series of four large oil paintings on Tibetan religious life — pilgrimage, prostration, chanting, and the sacred — included in the current donation. He later immigrated to California, where he works primarily as an art instructor while continuing his studio practice in figurative oil painting. Featured in an exclusive interview with Middle Land magazine.
